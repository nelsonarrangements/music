What PistonLink does

Everything you need at the console

Registration Profiles

Build named registration schemes with stop assignments across all ten General pistons — G1 through G10 — for Great, Swell, and Pedal. Name them anything ("Fast Sunday," "Christmas," "Closing Hymn") and every configuration you've ever built is a tap away.

Direct MIDI Deployment

Connect a MIDI adapter and tap Deploy. PistonLink writes your registrations directly to the organ's physical memory pistons over MIDI SysEx — the same as programming them at the console, but in seconds. Your profiles live in the organ even after you close the app.

Curated Registrations Library

A built-in library of stop suggestions for LDS hymns, organized by hymn number and style — verse-by-verse, general, pyramid, and ladder. Save any suggestion directly into a new or existing profile in one tap. Covers both the 1985 Hymnal and the 2025 For Home and Church collection.

Hands-Free Page Turns

Turn sheet music pages with a wink — right eye advances, left eye goes back. Powered by ARKit on iPad, calibrated to your face. No video is ever stored or transmitted; detection runs entirely on-device and lives only as long as the gesture takes.

Sheet Music Library & Setlists iPad

Import any PDF from Files or Mail, organize scores into setlists for each service, annotate with Apple Pencil, and link any score to a registration profile. Export your entire library as an archive for backup or transfer to another device.

Profile Sharing

Export any profile as a .pistonlink file and share it via AirDrop, Mail, or Messages. Recipients tap the file and it imports directly — no account required. Share a polished Christmas set with every organist in your stake in seconds.

How it works

From the app to the organ

01

Build Your Profile

Open the Console, select a piston, and tap stops to toggle them on. Switch between Great, Swell, and Pedal — assignments for each division are tracked independently. Stops are color-coded by family so your registration is visible at a glance. A 20-step undo history keeps experimentation low-risk.

02

Connect a MIDI Adapter

Plug a USB or Bluetooth 5-pin DIN adapter into your device and the organ's MIDI port. The app shows a live connection badge in the navigation — green means the organ is reachable, and the Deploy button stays disabled until an adapter is detected.

03

Deploy to the Organ

Tap Deploy, choose a memory slot (M2, M3, or M4), and confirm. The app steps through each piston in sequence, writing your stop selections to the organ's own memory via MIDI SysEx. A SysEx preview shows the exact data being sent for verification.

04

Play

Sit down and press your pistons. What you built in the app is exactly what the organ recalls — no guessing, no reprogramming from scratch, no hoping the last organist left something usable.

Questions

Common questions

What MIDI adapter do I need?

Any USB or Bluetooth 5-pin DIN MIDI adapter works. Common options include the Roland UM-ONE (wired) and the CME WIDI Master (Bluetooth wireless). The app shows a live connection badge so you'll know immediately whether it detected the adapter.

Does PistonLink work on iPhone?

Yes — building profiles, deploying registrations, and browsing the curated hymn library all work on iPhone. The sheet music Library, Setlists, hands-free page turning, and Apple Pencil annotation are iPad-only, since they rely on the larger screen and iPad-specific hardware.

What happens to my registrations if I lose my phone?

Nothing changes at the organ. Your registrations are written to the instrument's own memory, exactly as if you had programmed the pistons by hand at the console. The app is only needed to build and deploy profiles — once they're deployed, they live in the organ indefinitely.

What hymn collections are in the library?

The built-in library covers the LDS Hymnal (1985) and the newer For Home and Church (2025) collection. Each hymn includes verse-by-verse, general, pyramid, and ladder registration styles.

How does hands-free page turning work?

On iPad, PistonLink uses the TrueDepth camera and Apple's ARKit framework to detect eye winks. A right wink advances the page; a left wink goes back. Before first use, a short calibration step tunes the detection threshold to your face. No video is ever recorded, stored, or transmitted — detection runs entirely on-device and only lives long enough to register the gesture.

Can I share profiles with other organists?

Yes. Profiles export as .pistonlink files you can send via AirDrop, Mail, or Messages. Recipients tap the file to import it directly — no account, no setup. Useful for sharing a carefully-built Christmas set with everyone in your stake, or backing up your work to a new device.
